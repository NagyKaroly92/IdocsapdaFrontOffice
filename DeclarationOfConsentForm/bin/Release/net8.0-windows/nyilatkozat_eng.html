Ingredients
1 kg beef shank (and/or marrow bones)
1 kg beef brisket (and/or beef chuck)
5 medium carrots
3 medium parsnips
1 small celery root
1 small kohlrabi
1 medium onion
1 medium bell pepper
1 small cabbage
1 medium garlic bulb
1 medium tomato
1 bunch parsley
1 cherry pepper (optional)
150 g fine noodles
Salt to taste
Black pepper to taste
3 small mushrooms (optional)

Preparation
Peel the onion (just remove the outer layer), then cut it in half. Char the cut surface in the soup pot (or a pan lined with aluminum foil); this will give the soup a smokier, deeper flavor and a nicer color from the remaining skin.
Rinse the beef pieces and place them in the pot, then cover with 3 liters of cold water. You can also add whole black peppercorns. Bring to a boil, skim off the initial foam, and reduce the heat to low, continuing to simmer the soup.
As the meat begins to soften, add the cleaned and chopped vegetables, herbs, and cook until everything is tender - approximately 2.5-3 hours. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.
Once everything is cooked, remove the meat and vegetables from the pot. Remove the pot from heat and let it stand for 5 minutes to clarify, then strain through a very fine sieve or a sieve lined with cheesecloth into another container.
From the strained broth, scoop out enough to cook the noodles, and cook the fine noodles.
When serving, place the cooked vegetables and meat on a separate platter, ladle the hot soup, and garnish with fresh parsley if desired. You can also serve with spicy hot peppers (either fresh green or ground red).
